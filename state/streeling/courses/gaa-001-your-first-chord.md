# Your First Chord — E Minor

> **Guitar Alchemist Academy** | Stage: Nigredo (Beginner) | Duration: 20 minutes

## Objectives

After this lesson, you will be able to:
- Understand what a chord is — multiple notes sounding together
- Play the Em (E minor) chord cleanly on guitar
- Strum a basic down-strum pattern
- Transition between open strings and Em chord

---

## 1. What Is a Chord?

A chord is simply two or more notes played at the same time. When you strum all six strings of your guitar open (without pressing any frets), you're already playing a chord! It might not sound like much yet, but that's where we start.

Chords are the building blocks of songs. Most pop, rock, and folk songs use just 3-5 chords. By the end of this course, you'll know enough chords to play hundreds of songs.

### Practice Exercise

Strum all six open strings slowly, one at a time from low (thick) to high (thin). Then strum them all together. Listen to the sound — that's the open strings ringing together.

---

## 2. How to Read a Chord Diagram

A chord diagram is a picture of your guitar neck:

- **Vertical lines** = strings (low E on the left, high E on the right)
- **Horizontal lines** = frets
- **Black dots** = where to place your fingers
- **Numbers** = which finger to use (1=index, 2=middle, 3=ring, 4=pinky)
- **O** above a string = play it open (don't press anything)
- **X** above a string = don't play this string

For Em, all strings are played open except two — your fingers go on the 2nd fret of the A string and the 2nd fret of the D string.

---

## 3. Playing E Minor (Em)

Em is the easiest chord on guitar. Here's why: you only need two fingers, and all six strings ring out.

**Finger placement:**
1. Place your **middle finger (2)** on the **2nd fret** of the **A string** (5th string)
2. Place your **ring finger (3)** on the **2nd fret** of the **D string** (4th string)
3. All other strings are open — don't press anything else

**Tips:**
- Press down firmly, right behind the fret (not on top of it)
- Curve your fingers so you don't accidentally mute the neighboring strings
- Your thumb should rest behind the neck, roughly opposite your middle finger

### Practice Exercise

Place your fingers for Em. Strum slowly from the low E string (thickest) to the high E string (thinnest). Every string should ring clearly. If any string sounds buzzy or dead, adjust your finger pressure and position. Repeat 10 times.

---

## 4. Your First Strum Pattern

Now that you can hold the Em chord, let's make music with it.

**Basic down-strum pattern:**
- Strum DOWN across all strings, evenly and smoothly
- Count: 1 - 2 - 3 - 4 (one strum per beat)
- Keep a steady rhythm — use your foot to tap the beat
- Strum from your wrist, not your whole arm

**Once that's comfortable, try this pattern:**
- DOWN - DOWN - DOWN - DOWN (4 beats) → Em
- Lift fingers off → strum open strings (4 beats)
- Place fingers back → Em again (4 beats)

Congratulations — you're playing a chord progression!

### Practice Exercise

Set a timer for 2 minutes. Strum Em with steady down-strums, counting 1-2-3-4 out loud. Every 4 bars (16 beats), lift your fingers and strum open strings for 4 bars, then go back to Em. This builds your muscle memory for chord changes.

---

## 5. Why Em? The Alchemy Begins

We started with Em for a reason. In the alchemical tradition, the **Nigredo** (blackening) stage is about breaking down — letting go of what you think you know and starting from raw material.

Em is the rawest material on guitar: two fingers, six strings, a deep melancholic sound that has powered thousands of songs from Nirvana to Bob Marley. It's dark, it's simple, it's powerful.

**Next module**, we'll add Am (A minor) — and with just two chords, you'll be able to play your first real song.

## Self-Check Assessment

**1. How many fingers do you need to play Em?**
> Two — middle finger on A string 2nd fret, ring finger on D string 2nd fret

**2. When you strum Em, how many strings should ring out?**
> All six strings — Em is special, no strings are muted

**3. If a string sounds buzzy, what should you check?**
> Finger pressure (press harder) and position (press just behind the fret, not on top). Also check your fingers aren't touching neighboring strings.

**Pass criteria:** All six strings of the Em chord ring clearly without buzzing, and you can maintain a steady 4-beat strum pattern for 30 seconds.
