# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We, as contributors and maintainers of Demerzel, pledge to make participation in this community a safe, respectful, and intellectually honest experience for everyone — regardless of background, identity, or experience level.

This pledge is grounded in Demerzel's constitutional principles. The [Asimov Laws](constitutions/asimov.constitution.md) are not abstract: they apply here.

- **Zeroth Law** — do not harm humanity or the broader ecosystem of knowledge
- **First Law** — do not harm individual contributors (their safety, trust, or autonomy)
- **Second Law** — respect maintainer decisions and community norms
- **Third Law** — self-preservation is the lowest priority; community health comes first

---

## Our Standards

**Expected behavior:**

- Treat all participants with respect and good faith
- Prefer evidence over assertion; cite sources where possible
- Acknowledge uncertainty — say "I don't know" rather than speculate
- Escalate disagreements to maintainers rather than letting them fester
- Govern your contributions as you would govern an AI agent: transparently, proportionally, reversibly

**Unacceptable behavior:**

- Harassment, personal attacks, or discriminatory language of any kind
- Deliberate misinformation or bad-faith argumentation
- Publishing others' private information without consent
- Undermining constitutional principles (governance over heroics)
- Exploiting or obstructing the security disclosure process

---

## Tetravalent Conflict Resolution

Demerzel uses tetravalent logic (T/F/U/C) for reasoning about knowledge. We apply the same framework to interpersonal conflicts:

| State | Meaning | Action |
|-------|---------|--------|
| **T** (True) | Clear violation has occurred | Follow enforcement steps below |
| **F** (False) | Allegation is unfounded | Close with explanation |
| **U** (Unknown) | Situation is unclear | Investigate before acting — do not assume the worst |
| **C** (Contradictory) | Conflicting accounts | Escalate to project maintainers for mediation |

**Do not act on Unknown (U) as if it were True (T).** Investigation precedes judgment.

---

## Enforcement

Instances of unacceptable behavior may be reported by opening a private GitHub Security Advisory or contacting the maintainers directly. All reports will be treated confidentially.

Maintainers will:

1. Acknowledge the report within **48 hours**
2. Classify the situation using the tetravalent framework above
3. Respond proportionally — warnings before bans, reversible actions before permanent ones (Article 3: Reversibility)
4. Document the outcome in the governance log (Article 7: Auditability)

Consequences range from a written warning to permanent exclusion from the project.

---

## Governance Over Heroics

This community values **systematic governance over individual heroics**. Great contributions come from working within the framework — schemas, constitutions, behavioral tests — not around it. If the framework has a gap, the right response is to propose an amendment, not to bypass it.
